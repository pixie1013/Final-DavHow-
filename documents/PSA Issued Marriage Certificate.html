PSA Issued Marriage Certificate

The PSA Marriage Certificate is an official and legal document that provides information about a marriage. It includes details such as the names of the spouses, the date and place of the marriage, and the signatures of the witnesses. This certificate is considered a primary document for proving the validity of a marriage and is often required for various legal and official transactions, including changes in civil status, visa applications, and other government-related processes.

Online Application

1

Access the PSAHelpline.ph Website:

Go to the PSAHelpline.ph website homepage.

2

Initiate Order Process:

Click the "Order Now" button located on the right side of the screen or in the header.

3

Select Marriage Certificate:

On the next page, click the "Marriage" button.

Check the box at the bottom to accept the terms and conditions, then click "Continue."

4

Choose Request Type:

Choose whether you're requesting your own PSA marriage certificate or for someone else.

Click "Continue" to proceed.

5

Provide Personal Information:

Enter your personal details such as sex, civil status (if female), first name, middle name, last name, birth date, and the type of your government-issued ID.

Click "Continue" after completing the form.

6

Enter Spouse's Details:

Input your spouse’s details, including their first name, middle name, and last name.

Click “Continue” to proceed.

7

Specify Marriage Date:

Input the date of your marriage.

Click “Continue” to proceed.

8

Indicate Place of Marriage:

Select the country, province, and municipality where your marriage took place.

Click “Continue” to proceed.

9

Select Purpose for Requesting:

Specify your purpose for requesting a copy of your marriage certificate.

Click “Continue” to proceed.

10

Provide Delivery Information:

Finally, provide your delivery address, mobile number, and email address.

Walk in Application

1

Set Appointment:

Visit https://appointment.psa.gov.ph/ to schedule an appointment for your document request.

2

Visit CRS Outlet on Appointment Date:

Arrive at the PSA CRS Outlet on the scheduled date and time of your appointment.

3

Present CRS Appointment Slip and Valid ID:

Present the printed or electronic copy of the CRS Appointment Slip along with one valid ID.

Clients with PhilID/ePhilID requesting a Death Certificate for immediate family members can transact without an appointment.

4

For Authorized Representative:

If you're an authorized representative, ensure you have:

• Valid Identity Document (ID) of the document owner (original and photocopy) with a clear photo, full name in print, signature, and issued by an official authority.

• Your own valid ID.

• CRS Appointment Slip bearing the name of the authorized representative.

5

If Request is at PSA Civil Registry Outlet Hosted by LGU:

If the request for a copy issuance of a civil registry document will be availed at the PSA Civil Registry Outlet hosted by the Local Government Unit (LGU):

• Present the printed or electronic copy of the CRS Appointment Slip.

• Provide the original OR from the Local Government Unit (LGU) as proof of payment of the LGU Add-on Fee.

• Present one valid ID upon entry at the PSA Civil Registry System Outlet.

6

Health and Safety Protocols:

Pass through health and safety protocols, including allowing the Updated QR Code of the COVID19 Digital Contact Tracing App to be scanned or filling up and submitting the Health Survey Form.

Undergo temperature check.

7

Get Queue Ticket Number (QTN):

Obtain a Queue Ticket Number (QTN) upon entry at the outlet.

8

Fill Out Application Form (AF):

Get an Application Form for Marriage Certificate (Pink Form) from the AF dispenser.

Fill out the required information in printed letters on the form.

9

Screening Area:

Proceed to the screening area and present the AF and required documents to the screener for completeness check.

First Time Job Seekers should proceed to the Public Assistance and Complaints Desk (PACD).

10

Payment Window:

Proceed to the payment window and submit the AF and required documents.

Pay the corresponding fee for the request, either in cash or through online cashless payment.

Check the details in the issued Official Receipt (OR).

Make sure to count the change before leaving the counter.

11

Proceed to the Releasing Area:

After payment, proceed to the Releasing Area and wait while the request is being processed.

12

Wait for Name to be Called:

Wait for the name of the document owner/client to be called.

13

Proceed to Releasing Window:

Once your name is called, proceed to the Releasing Window where the document owner/client’s name was called.

14

Present the ORIGINAL OR and Other Requirements:

Present the ORIGINAL OR and other requirements at the Releasing Window.

First Time Jobseekers should present their claim stub at the Releasing Window.

15

Check the Document:

Carefully examine the received document to ensure all information is accurate and complete.

16

Sign the “Received by” Portion:

Sign the “Received by” portion at the back of the AF and indicate the date of receipt.

First Time Jobseekers should also sign in the control logbook.
